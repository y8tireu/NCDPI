4th Grade Math

Multi-digit Operations

Practice standard algorithms for addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. For example, to solve 37 + 85, add the ones (7+5=12, write 2, carry 1) then add the tens.

  • Addition & Subtraction with Regrouping
  • Multi-digit Multiplication (partial products)
  • Long Division (divide, multiply, subtract, bring down)

Sample Problem: 5,742 + 3,189 = 8,931

Practice on: Khan Academy & IXL.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: Solve 4,125 – 2,389.
    A: 1,736
  • Q: Multiply 123 × 45 using partial products.
    A: 5,535
  • Q: Divide 864 by 6.
    A: 144
  • Q: Find the missing number: ? + 678 = 1,234.
    A: 556
  • Q: What is 7,500 – 3,268?
    A: 4,232
  • Q: Multiply 87 × 32.
    A: 2,784

Fractions

Work on equivalent fractions, comparing fractions with common denominators, and performing operations.

  • Understanding numerators and denominators
  • Finding equivalent fractions (e.g., 3/4 = 6/8)
  • Comparing fractions using common denominators

Sample Problem: Which is greater: 2/3 or 3/5?

Practice on: Khan Academy Fractions & IXL.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: Simplify 8/12.
    A: 2/3
  • Q: Find an equivalent fraction for 5/6.
    A: 10/12
  • Q: Order these fractions (least to greatest): 3/4, 2/5, 5/8.
    A: 2/5, 5/8, 3/4
  • Q: If you have 1/2 of a cake and eat 1/4, what remains?
    A: 1/4 remains
  • Q: What is 3/4 + 1/4?
    A: 1
  • Q: Subtract: 5/6 – 1/3.
    A: 5/6 – 2/6 = 1/2

Geometry (Angles, Shapes, Area, Perimeter)

Focus on measuring angles, classifying shapes, and calculating area and perimeter.

  • Identify acute, right, and obtuse angles
  • Classify shapes: squares, rectangles, trapezoids
  • Area = length × width; Perimeter = sum of sides

Sample Problem: A rectangle 7 m long and 3 m wide has area 21 m² and perimeter 20 m.

Practice on: Khan Academy & SplashLearn.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: Using a protractor, if an angle measures 35°, what type is it?
    A: Acute
  • Q: List three properties that define a square.
    A: Four equal sides, four right angles, and parallel opposite sides.
  • Q: Find the perimeter of a triangle with sides 5 cm, 7 cm, and 8 cm.
    A: 20 cm
  • Q: A rectangle has an area of 36 m² and a length of 9 m. What is its width?
    A: 4 m
  • Q: What is the area of a square with a side length of 6 units?
    A: 36 square units
  • Q: If a triangle's base is 4 cm and its height is 5 cm, what is the area?
    A: 10 cm²

Measurement & Data

Cover unit conversions, volume calculations, and data interpretation from graphs.

  • Volume = length × width × height
  • Convert between units (inches to feet, meters to centimeters)
  • Interpret bar graphs and line plots

Sample Problem: Convert 36 inches to feet (36 ÷ 12 = 3 feet).

Practice on: IXL & worksheets on K5 Learning.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: A rectangular prism has a base area of 24 cm² and a height of 5 cm. What is its volume?
    A: 120 cm³
  • Q: Convert 3.5 meters to centimeters.
    A: 350 cm
  • Q: If a line plot shows values 1.2, 1.5, 1.8, and 2.0, what is the range?
    A: 0.8
  • Q: If 8 oranges weigh 2 kg, what is the weight of 12 oranges?
    A: 3 kg
  • Q: Convert 500 ml to liters.
    A: 0.5 liters
  • Q: A chart shows scores: 72, 85, 85, 90, 90, 90, 95. What is the mode?
    A: 90

Algebraic Thinking

Practice writing and evaluating expressions, solving simple equations, and recognizing numerical patterns.

  • Translate phrases into expressions (e.g., "2x – 7")
  • Evaluate expressions for given values
  • Solve simple equations (e.g., x + 8 = 15)

Sample Problem: If y = 4x + 1 and x = 5, then y = 21.

Practice on: Khan Academy & IXL.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: Translate "7 less than 2 times a number" into an expression.
    A: 2x – 7
  • Q: What is the next number in the pattern: 4, 9, 14, ___?
    A: 19
  • Q: Solve for x: x + 8 = 15.
    A: x = 7
  • Q: Evaluate: 3(4) + 2(5) – 7.
    A: 15
  • Q: Write an expression for "three more than four times a number."
    A: 4x + 3
  • Q: Identify the pattern in: 2, 5, 8, 11, __.
    A: Next is 14 (add 3 each time).

Multi-Step Word Problems

Solve word problems that require multiple steps and integration of several operations.

Sample Problem: A cafeteria makes 7 pizzas (8 slices each) and has 18 slices left. Slices eaten = 56 – 18 = 38, which equals 4 pizzas and 6 extra slices.

Practice on: Khan Academy & word problem sets on Math-Aids.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: A farmer has 40 m of fencing for a rectangular field. If the length is 12 m, what is the width?
    A: 8 m
  • Q: In a class of 30, 3/5 passed a test. How many passed?
    A: 18 students
  • Q: A shop sells pencils in packs of 10. How many packs for 125 pencils?
    A: 13 packs
  • Q: Mary had $50, spent $12 on a book and $18 on supplies. How much is left?
    A: $20
  • Q: A car travels 240 miles in 4 hours. What is its average speed?
    A: 60 mph
  • Q: If a recipe calls for 2/3 cup of sugar and you double the recipe, how much sugar is needed?
    A: 4/3 or 1 1/3 cups

5th Grade Math

Fostering Mathematical Excellence in 5th Grade: A Comparative Overview

The transition into 5th grade marks a critical juncture in a student’s academic journey. At this stage, learners advance from basic arithmetic to more complex concepts that build the foundation for higher-level problem solving. By emphasizing reasoning, analytical thinking, and real-world application, the curriculum prepares students for the challenges of middle school and beyond.

In North Carolina, the math curriculum is designed to develop a deep conceptual understanding. Instruction moves from mastering standard operations to exploring geometry, data interpretation, and introductory algebra. This ensures that students not only perform computations but also grasp the underlying principles.

Massachusetts, with its rigorous End-of-Grade assessments, aligns classroom instruction closely with state standards. The Massachusetts 5th Grade Math EOG guides provide educators with clear strategies to prepare students for standardized testing while ensuring learning remains comprehensive and engaging.

New York’s dynamic curriculum—reflected in updated documents for 2022, 2023, and 2024—adapts to the latest educational research and best practices. This constant evolution ensures that instruction stays current and effective.

In conclusion, these diverse state approaches to 5th grade mathematics share a common goal: empowering students with robust mathematical skills and confidence. A well-rounded curriculum, continuous assessment, and periodic updates lay the groundwork for lifelong academic success.

5th Grade ELA

Advanced Reading Comprehension

Analyze longer texts by identifying key details, making inferences, and summarizing main ideas.

  • Analyze text beyond its literal meaning
  • Make deep inferences and summarize
  • Compare texts and discuss differing perspectives

Practice on: ReadWorks & CommonLit.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: After reading a passage, list three details that support its main idea.
    A: (Answers vary; e.g., "setting description, character actions, and a key event.")
  • Q: What inference can you make from "He slammed the door, his face flushed with anger"?
    A: The character is extremely angry.
  • Q: Write a brief summary of a narrative about overcoming a challenge.
    A: (Example) "The narrative details a character who faces obstacles but perseveres to achieve success."
  • Q: Predict what might happen next based on the title and first paragraph.
    A: (Example) "The conflict will escalate, leading to a surprising twist."
  • Q: Identify the tone of a passage describing a stormy night with gloomy language.
    A: Gloomy and ominous.
  • Q: Compare two passages about the same event. What is one key difference in their perspectives?
    A: One may be subjective (first-person) and the other is objective (third-person).

Literary & Informational Text Analysis

Analyze figurative language, author’s purpose, tone, and mood in both narrative and informational texts.

  • Identify similes, metaphors, and personification
  • Determine the author’s purpose (persuade, inform, entertain)
  • Analyze tone, mood, and point of view

Explore on: IXL ELA & Flocabulary.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: Find a metaphor in a poem and explain its meaning.
    A: (Example) "Time is a thief" means time steals moments from our lives.
  • Q: What is the author’s purpose in a persuasive essay about recycling?
    A: To convince readers to recycle by presenting facts and benefits.
  • Q: Determine the tone of a narrative that uses dark, descriptive language.
    A: Somber and reflective.
  • Q: Compare the point of view in two passages about the same historical event.
    A: One is first-person (personal) and the other is third-person (objective).
  • Q: Identify the figurative language in "Her smile was as bright as the sun."
    A: A simile comparing her smile to the sun.

Grammar & Vocabulary

Practice using context clues, understanding Greek/Latin roots, and editing sentences.

  • Identify parts of speech and root words
  • Edit sentences for proper punctuation and capitalization
  • Create complex and compound sentences

Practice on: Education.com & IXL.

Additional NC EOG Sample Questions

  • Q: Identify the root word in "transportation" and list two related words.
    A: Root: "port" (carry); Related: "portable," "import."
  • Q: Correct this sentence: "me and my brother went to the park."
    A: "My brother and I went to the park."
  • Q: Underline the context clue in "The jubilant crowd cheered as the team scored."
    A: "Jubilant" indicates the crowd was extremely happy.
  • Q: Create one complex sentence using "although."
    A: "Although it was raining, we enjoyed the picnic."
  • Q: Rewrite "i cant wait to go to summer camp" with correct grammar.
    A: "I can't wait to go to summer camp."
  • Q: What does the prefix "un-" mean in "unhappy"?
    A: "Not."
